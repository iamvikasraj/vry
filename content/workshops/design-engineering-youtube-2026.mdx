A recorded design engineering workshop for Design Originals Club — motion, systems thinking, and building interactions that hold up in production.

## Overview

This session walks through how I approach design engineering work: starting from user intent, mapping states, then choosing the right tool for the prototype. We use a real product example (placeholder) to show the full loop from sketch to shippable interaction.

## What you'll learn

**Interaction audits** — spotting where UI loses clarity or trust.

**Rive workflows** — artboards, inputs, and exporting for web or mobile.

**Design systems** — reusing motion and layout patterns across features.

## Recording

The full session is available on YouTube: [Design Engineering Workshop](https://youtu.be/fTUbis6k8w8?si=jsjvtDXCJ_Hg7oMP).

## Tools

**Rive** — state machines and animation  
**Figma** — layout and component specs  
**Design systems** — tokens, variants, documentation
